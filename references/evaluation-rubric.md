# Evaluation Rubric

Use this rubric to keep graduate-application judgments consistent. It is a decision aid, not a fake scoring system.

## 1. Academic Competitiveness

Check:

- Undergraduate school context
- GPA and grade trend
- Rank percentile if available
- Relevant coursework strength

Quick interpretation:

- Strong: academic base clearly supports the target tier
- Medium: acceptable but not a strong differentiator
- Weak: GPA, course rigor, or school context will materially limit options

## 2. Program Fit

Check:

- Alignment between current background and target program
- Relevance of internships, projects, labs, or coursework
- Whether the user's story makes sense for the target direction

Quick interpretation:

- Strong: direct and coherent fit
- Medium: reasonable fit with some gaps
- Weak: major pivot without enough evidence

## 3. Experience Depth

Check:

- Internships
- Research
- Projects
- Competitions
- Publications, patents, or shipped outcomes if any

Quick interpretation:

- Strong: clear depth with ownership and outputs
- Medium: some relevant items but limited depth
- Weak: generic or weakly related experience

## 4. Test Coverage

Check:

- TOEFL, IELTS, or Duolingo
- GRE or GMAT if relevant

Quick interpretation:

- Strong: scores already clear likely thresholds
- Medium: usable but not a strength
- Weak: missing or below likely thresholds

## 5. Narrative Strength

Check:

- Why this field
- Why now
- Why this background leads logically to the next step
- Whether post-graduation goals are coherent

Quick interpretation:

- Strong: coherent story and clear trajectory
- Medium: reasonable but generic
- Weak: obvious narrative gaps or unclear motive

## 6. Risk Flags

Common flags:

- Very low GPA for target tier
- Cross-major without technical or academic foundation
- No research for research-heavy programs
- No quantified outcomes in experience
- Missing language score close to deadlines
- School list concentrated in one overly ambitious band

## Compact Scoring Method

Use only when it helps clarify the answer. Do not pretend precision.

- Strong = 3
- Medium = 2
- Weak = 1

Suggested interpretation:

- 13-15: can support some ambitious applications if fit is real
- 10-12: balanced, target-heavy list is safer
- 7-9: list should become more conservative or more specialized
- 5-6: major recalibration needed before finalizing the plan

Always override the rough score if one structural issue dominates, such as missing tests or a major-fit mismatch.
